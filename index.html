UX Skills

Empathy

My approach to user empathy is to focus on what the user is telling me and leave my assumptions aside. I then reflect and analyze my findings with an empathy map and/or persona to better understand the user’s goals and needs.

Prototyping

Prototyping is such an essential part to UX design, and it also happens to be my favorite part. I enjoy creating mockups of what the final product(s) is going to look like and receiving testing feedback from users. After all, it’s about seeing how effective a product is for the user.

Information Architecture

The ability to organize information in an understandable manner is critical. Information architecture quickly became my second favorite part of the design process because creating a product that is effective and user friendly is what I always strive for. It’s where design and technology merge to make lives easier.

Work
Ace Travel: A Mobile Travel App

Currently, people have a hard time planning and budgeting for trips and use more than one app/website to do so. Ace travel was designed to allows users to plan, coordinate, and budget for trips all from one place. I focused on the on-boarding process and overall navigation of the app.

Hope for Children Responsive Web Design

Hope for Children Foundation was designed to educate, train, and receive donations from website visitors but due its unreliable aesthetic and confusing navigation, users were unable to complete tasks. Information architecture and modernizing the look and feel of the desktop and mobile version were attacked during this project.

Bridge: A Mobile Community Donation App

Bridge was designed to allow users to find those in need of donations within their community vs always donating to organizations that already receive a lot of help. This app increases the impact of donations made and brings great satisfactions to users.
